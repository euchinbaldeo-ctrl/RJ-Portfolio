01 / FEATURED PROJECT

USWAG: A Proposed Civic Landmark Municipal Complex

Civic Architecture — Thesis Project

A municipal government complex proposed for Barangay Dayao, Mandaon, Masbate, USWAG reimagines the local municipal hall as both a functional seat of governance and a civic landmark for the community. The design uses sweeping, curvilinear forms and a continuous wave-like roofline to reflect Mandaon's coastal identity, while an elevated pedestrian bridge links the hall to an adjacent covered gathering pavilion, encouraging movement and interaction across the complex.

A rooftop garden and integrated solar panel array reinforce the project's commitment to climate-responsive, sustainable civic design, while warm wood-slat and copper-toned louvers soften the building's institutional scale and ground it in a distinctly Filipino architectural language. Surrounding landscaped plazas, government buildings, parking, and bicycle-friendly streetscapes extend the building's public presence outward, positioning USWAG as a symbol of progress for the municipality.

02 / RESIDENTIAL

Hillside Retreat House

Private Residence — Design Studio IV

A weekend house designed for a sloped lot in Tagaytay, organized around a central courtyard that frames views of the ridge. The section was driven by topography — each floor steps back into the hillside, minimizing excavation and maximizing passive cooling.

03 / ADAPTIVE REUSE

The Old Mill Collective

Mixed-Use Adaptive Reuse — Urban Design Studio

Conversion of an early 20th-century rice mill into a mixed-use complex of studios, a café, and a maker workshop. The project prioritized revealing original industrial structure — exposed trusses, brick walls — as the primary aesthetic language of the intervention.

Threshold — Pavilion Design

Temporary Pavilion — Architecture Week 2024

A temporary pavilion built for an inter-school architecture festival, exploring the spatial quality of threshold — the in-between moment of entering and leaving. Constructed from bent rebar and translucent polycarbonate sheeting by a team of five students over one week.

Design Process

How I approach
every project

I

Listen & Observe

Every project begins with deep site analysis and understanding the people who will inhabit the space. I read the land before putting pencil to paper.

II

Sketch & Ideate

Hand drawing remains my primary thinking tool. Loose, fast, iterative — I fill sketchbooks before committing to any digital model.

III

Model & Test

Physical study models and digital tools are used in parallel to test massing, light, and structure. I treat models as questions, not answers.

IV

Refine & Present

Technical drawings, renders, and diagrams are crafted to communicate clearly — not just to impress, but to build shared understanding with collaborators and clients.
