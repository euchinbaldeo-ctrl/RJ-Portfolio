01 / FEATURED PROJECT

USWAG: A Proposed Civic Landmark Municipal Complex

Civic Architecture — Thesis Project

A municipal government complex proposed for Barangay Dayao, Mandaon, Masbate, USWAG reimagines the local municipal hall as both a functional seat of governance and a civic landmark for the community. The design uses sweeping, curvilinear forms and a continuous wave-like roofline to reflect Mandaon's coastal identity.

A rooftop garden and integrated solar panel array reinforce the project's commitment to climate-responsive, sustainable civic design. At the same time, warm wood-slat and copper-toned louvers soften the building's institutional scale and ground it in a distinctly Filipino architectural language. Surrounding landscaped plazas, government buildings, parking, and bicycle-friendly streetscapes extend the building's public presence outward, positioning USWAG as a symbol of progress for the municipality.

Urban Design Civic Sustainable Thesis
02 / FEATURED PROJECT

Arqora Corporation

COMMERCIAL ARCHITECTURE — OFFICE BUILDING

Arqora Corp. is a proposed six-story office building designed to serve as the headquarters of an architectural firm. The building is composed of two main volumes: a lower entrance block with full-height glass curtain walls that highlight the internal staircase, and a taller office wing located behind it. The office wing features a gently curved façade and repeated floor-to-ceiling windows that create a clean and modern appearance.

On the left side of the building, timber-clad vertical fins and an open-air balcony add warmth and texture, creating a contrast with the smooth stucco finish of the upper floors. The extensive use of glass improves transparency, allows natural daylight to enter the building, and strengthens the connection between the workspace and its surroundings. The design combines a professional corporate identity with a welcoming architectural character, reflecting the creativity, innovation, and collaborative nature of the firm it represents.

Commercial Office Institutional
03 / FEATURED PROJECT

A proposed china town complex

HIGH-RISE ARCHITECTURE — MIXED-USE DEVELOPMENT

Located in Legazpi, the Celestial Tower is a 50-story mixed-use high-rise designed to integrate commercial, office, and residential spaces within a single vertical development. The lower five floors house commercial spaces, while the floors above transition into office space, marked by incremental setbacks that reduce the building's mass as it rises. The upper levels feature residential units and a hotel, including a private penthouse and a rooftop helipad.

The tower’s diagrid façade wraps the entire structure in a diamond lattice pattern, creating a distinct silhouette while providing structural support. A central core extends from the basement to the highest floor, reinforcing the building’s stability and efficiency. A curved podium base, finished with wave-like patterns and warm wood accents, anchors the tower to the ground and distinguishes the public entrance from the residential and office levels above.

Design Process

How I approach
every project

I

Listen & Observe

Every project begins with deep site analysis and understanding the people who will inhabit the space. I read the land before putting pencil to paper.

II

Sketch & Ideate

Hand drawing remains my primary thinking tool. Loose, fast, iterative — I fill sketchbooks before committing to any digital model.

III

Model & Test

Physical study models and digital tools are used in parallel to test massing, light, and structure. I treat models as questions, not answers.

IV

Refine & Present

Technical drawings, renders, and diagrams are crafted to communicate clearly — not just to impress, but to build shared understanding with collaborators and clients.
